1 · Category Diagnosis

The sharp part. Each finding names what is broken, the evidence behind it, and what it costs — then points to the 4P move that fixes it. Severity is capped by evidence grade: a claim on thin evidence is downgraded to Watch and routed to the test queue, never dressed up as a settled conclusion.

Critical L1 No SKU in the fastest-growing premium sub-band
The 1,200–1,800 PLN sub-band is where premium growth concentrates, yet the portfolio jumps straight from Aurora Pro (entry-premium) to Aurora Max (flagship), leaving the band empty. Premium Rival P owns it uncontested.
Evidence: Own + competitor price listings across the three largest local marketplaces · sourced
Cost if ignored: The single fastest-growing band is conceded by default; share captured here compounds as the category premiumizes.
→ Position Aurora Pro into the 1,200–1,800 band, or introduce a Pro variant — do not leave the band to one competitor.
Major L2 Volume concentrated in the shrinking entry tier
Two of six SKUs sit in the entry tier, which is shrinking under a price war while the mid and premium tiers grow. The portfolio's center of gravity is in the wrong place for where the category is heading.
Evidence: Category tier-trend read from marketplace assortment and price-drop frequency · assumed
Cost if ignored: Effort and inventory anchored to a declining tier; margin erodes as the price war deepens.
→ Harvest entry SKUs for margin, shift new investment to mid and premium.
Major L3 A3 and A3 Plus cannibalize each other
Aurora A3 and A3 Plus occupy the same price point and the same mid-tier position. Channels are forced to pick one; the two split demand and weaken both listings' review velocity.
Evidence: Own product catalog + marketplace pricing (both live at near-identical price) · sourced
Cost if ignored: Self-inflicted split of mid-tier demand; neither SKU builds the review mass that wins the band.
→ Consolidate on A3 as the mid-tier hero; plan A3 Plus exit and clear inventory.
Major L5 Entry SKUs over-concentrated on one marketplace
Both entry SKUs lean on a single marketplace for visibility, while a meaningful share of first-time buyers in this category discover via bundled retail and telco offers where Aurora is absent.
Evidence: Channel presence audit vs typical category purchase paths · assumed
Cost if ignored: Concentration risk plus missed discovery surface; one marketplace algorithm change can wipe entry-tier visibility.
→ Diversify entry distribution into bundle/retail; treat the single marketplace as one lever, not the whole shelf.
Major L6 Flagship Aurora Max is invisible at launch
Aurora Max is the brand's premium flagship but has no localized product page and zero reviews on the main marketplace — buyers searching the premium band cannot find it or trust it.
Evidence: Localized PDP absent; review count = 0 (both directly verifiable) · sourced
Cost if ignored: The SKU meant to establish premium brand altitude is silent exactly where high-consideration buyers decide.
→ Build a localized PDP, seed expert reviews, and lead with technical media — do not discount the flagship.
Watch L4 Aurora Pro may be stuck in a permanent-promo spiral
Aurora Pro appears to carry a near-constant discount, which would erode its anchor price and make full-price sell-through impossible — a classic premium-killing spiral.
Evidence: Observed promo-banner frequency on listing pages (no access to actual transaction price or margin) · hypothesis · severity capped — evidence is hypothesis, enters test queue
Cost if ignored: If true, the premium anchor is already damaged; if false, no action needed. Cannot tell without transaction-price data.
→ Pull a 90-day transaction-price history; if permanent-discount confirmed, freeze promo and rebuild the anchor.
